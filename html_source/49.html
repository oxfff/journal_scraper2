      • Competitive classroom norms and exclusion of children with academic and behavior difficulties

        Original Research Article
      • Pages 1-11
      • Luciano Gasser, Jeanine Grütter, Loredana Torchetti, Alois Buholzer
        • Abstract
        • Close research highlights
        •  PDF (762 K)

        Highlights

        •

        1009 children were investigated within a two-wave study (fifth and sixth grade).

        •

        Children exclude more hypothetical hyperactive than low-achieving peers.

        •

        With grade children increasingly consider the context of exclusion.

        •

        Classroom competitive norms predict the exclusion of low-achieving children.

        •

        Teachers can encourage children to integrate academic goals and fairness principles.

      • One of the family? Measuring young adolescents' relationships with pets and siblings

        Original Research Article
      • Pages 12-20
      • Matthew T Cassels, Naomi White, Nancy Gee, Claire Hughes
        • Abstract
        • Close research highlights
        •  PDF (585 K)

        Highlights

        •

        Psychometric validity of a pet adaptation of the Network of Relationships Inventory was shown.

        •

        Girls reported more intimate disclosure, companionship, and conflict with their pet than did boys.

        •

        Dog owners reported more satisfaction and companionship with their pet than owners of other pets.

        •

        Children reported more satisfaction and less conflict with their pets than with their siblings.

      • Exposure to violence during adolescence as a predictor of perceived stress trajectories in emerging adulthood

        Original Research Article
      • Pages 31-38
      • Justin E. Heinze, Sarah A. Stoddard, Sophie M. Aiyer, Andria B. Eisman, Marc A. Zimmerman
        • Abstract
        • Close research highlights
        •  PDF (343 K)

        Highlights

        •

        Overall, violence exposure by year decreased as adolescents aged along with perceived stress.

        •

        Changes in violence and perceived stress varied between individuals.

        •

        Baseline and increases in cumulative violence exposure predicted perceived stress at age 20 and over time.

        •

        Females reported higher stress levels at the start of emerging adulthood, but also faster decreases over time.

        •

        Perceived stress did not differ by race/ethnicity.
